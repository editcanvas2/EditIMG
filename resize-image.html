Image Resize Tool

Change the width and height of your images in pixels with a live preview.

How to Resize Your Image

  1. Upload Image: Drop your image file into the upload area or click to select it.
  2. Enter New Dimensions: In the options panel on the left, type your desired width or height in the input boxes.
  3. Maintain Aspect Ratio: Keep the "Maintain aspect ratio" box checked to prevent your image from being stretched or distorted. Uncheck it to set width and height independently.
  4. Download Your Image: Once you are happy with the preview on the right, click the "Download Image" button to save the resized version.

Resizing FAQ

Making an image smaller (downscaling) generally preserves quality well. Making an image significantly larger (upscaling) than its original size can cause it to lose sharpness and appear blurry or pixelated, as the browser has to guess what the new pixels should look like.

Aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between an image's width and height. Keeping it locked ensures that when you change one dimension (e.g., width), the other dimension (height) adjusts automatically to keep the image from looking squashed.
